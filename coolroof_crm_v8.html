⚖️ Terms & Conditions
1. SCOPE OF WORK
Work performed is limited strictly to the services listed in this estimate. Any additional work requires a separate written Change Order. Contractor is not responsible for pre-existing structural issues, interior damage, rot, or code violations unless separately quoted in writing.

2. PAYMENT TERMS
Deposit: 10% upon signing · Progress: 40% at job midpoint · Final: 50% upon completion. Accepted: Credit Card, ACH, Check, Cash. Invoices unpaid after 30 days subject to 1.5% monthly late fee (18% APR). All warranties void if payment not received in full. Client agrees to pay attorney's fees in event of collection action (CA Civil Code §1717).

3. PROJECT TIMELINE
Start date subject to permit approval, weather, and material availability. Contractor not liable for delays from weather, material shortages, permit delays, labor disputes, or circumstances beyond Contractor's control.

4. MATERIALS
All materials as specified in estimate. Contractor reserves right to substitute materials of equal or greater quality if specified materials become unavailable. Material color/style changes after ordering may incur Change Order charges.

5. CHANGE ORDERS
Any changes to original scope must be agreed in writing via signed Change Order before additional work begins. Verbal agreements are not binding. Additional wood/deck repair rates: Plywood/OSB $144/sheet · 1×4–1×8 board $4.50/ln ft · 2×4–2×10 rafter $8.00/ln ft · Fascia (2×8–2×12) $18.00/ln ft · Extra membrane layer removal $40/100 sqft.

6. WARRANTY
Workmanship Warranty: 2 years from project completion. Manufacturer Warranty: per product specifications. Warranty is VOID if: (a) full payment not received; (b) unauthorized repairs made by others; (c) damage from storm, hail, acts of God, or improper maintenance; (d) Client fails routine roof maintenance. Warranty does not cover interior damages, pre-existing conditions, or issues unrelated to work performed. Warranty is transferable upon written notice to Contractor within 30 days of property ownership transfer.

7. OPEN ROOF CLAUSE — READ CAREFULLY
Roofing work requires temporary removal of existing materials, exposing the structure to the open environment ("Open Roof Period"). Contractor will make every commercially reasonable effort to protect the open structure with tarps during non-working hours or when inclement weather is anticipated. However, Contractor shall NOT be held liable for water intrusion, interior damage, or property damage of any kind from rain, wind, hail, or weather events during the Open Roof Period. Client acknowledges this risk, is advised to protect valuables, and is advised to notify their insurance provider prior to project start. If structural deficiencies are discovered upon tear-off, Contractor will notify Client and issue a Change Order before proceeding. Force Majeure: Contractor's liability during sudden weather events is limited to cost of temporary protection only.

8. PROPERTY & SITE CONDITIONS
Client responsible for removing fragile items, vehicles, and valuables near work area before start. Contractor NOT responsible for damage to landscaping, driveways, gutters, satellite dishes, antennas, or interior items (nail pops, drywall cracks, dust) from normal roofing operations. All debris will be removed and property swept with magnetic roller upon completion.

9. CANCELLATION POLICY
Client may cancel within 3 business days of signing for full deposit refund (CA Civil Code §1689.5). After 3 business days, deposit non-refundable if materials ordered. After work begins, Client responsible for all labor/material costs incurred plus 25% of remaining contract value as liquidated damages.

10. INSURANCE & LICENSING
Contractor holds valid CA Contractor's License #920169 (CSLB). Contractor maintains General Liability Insurance and Workers' Compensation coverage per CA law. Certificate of insurance available upon request. Contractor is bonded per CA Business & Professions Code §7071.6.

11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
Contractor's total liability shall not exceed the total amount paid under this contract. Contractor not liable for indirect, consequential, or special damages. Claims must be submitted in writing within 7 days of discovery and filed within 1 year of accrual (CA Code of Civil Procedure).

12. MOLD & HAZARDOUS MATERIALS
This contract does not include mold remediation, asbestos removal, or hazardous material abatement. If discovered during work, these will be quoted separately as a Change Order per CA OSHA regulations.

13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Disputes first submitted to good-faith mediation. If unresolved within 30 days, disputes proceed to binding arbitration (CA CCP §1280 et seq.). Governed by California law. Venue in county where work was performed. Prevailing party entitled to attorney's fees (CA Civil Code §1717). Nothing herein limits consumer rights under CSLB (B&P Code §7000 et seq.).

14. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
This estimate and terms constitute the entire agreement. No verbal agreements are binding. Any modifications must be in writing and signed by both parties. If any provision is found invalid under CA law, remaining provisions remain in full force.
